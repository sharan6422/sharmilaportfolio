My Philosophy

Curiosity is my creative process. I ask questions until I find the real problem. I research until I understand the people behind it. I connect ideas that don't seem connected. I build strategies that balance relevance with originality. And I create work that is rooted in insight, not assumptions.

Because the best ideas rarely come from the first answer. They come from being curious enough to keep asking why.

01 — Discovery

I don't start with ideas. I start with questions.

Most people see a brief and immediately think about logos, campaigns, or content. My first instinct is to ask questions. Lots of them.

  • Why does this brand exist?
  • Who is it trying to help?
  • What problem is it solving?
  • Why does that problem exist in the first place?


And then I keep asking why until I reach something deeper than the obvious answer.

For example, if someone came to me wanting to launch a short kurti brand, I wouldn't immediately think about colors, packaging, or Instagram posts. I would ask:
Why create another kurti brand?

Maybe the answer is: "To give women more options for modern ethnic wear."

Then I ask: Why do women need more options?
Maybe because many fast-fashion brands have made fashion accessible, but also repetitive. I call this the "Zudio Effect." Most people who shop at Zudio have experienced walking into a mall, college campus, or café and spotting multiple people wearing the exact same outfit. The clothes are affordable and trendy, but they often lack individuality.

Then I ask: Why is that a problem?
Because fashion isn't just clothing. It's self-expression. Most people don't want to accidentally match with strangers every time they step outside.

Then I ask: Why kurtis specifically?
Maybe because the founder wants to celebrate Indian heritage while creating something modern and wearable for everyday life.

Now we've moved from: "We sell kurtis."
To: "We help women express individuality through modern Indian fashion while staying connected to their cultural roots."

That's a completely different brand story. And it all came from asking better questions.

02 — Research

Understanding people before trying to sell to them.

I believe the best marketing decisions are informed by both data and human behaviour. That's why I spend a lot of time researching. Not just statistics and reports. People.

I read:

  • Research papers
  • Industry articles
  • Consumer studies
  • Reddit discussions
  • Instagram comments
  • Product reviews
  • YouTube comment sections
  • Community forums


Because people are often more honest in comments and reviews than they are in surveys.

For example, if I'm studying a beauty brand, I don't just want to know the demographics. I want to know:

  • What frustrates people?
  • What excites them?
  • What do they complain about repeatedly?
  • What language do they use naturally?
  • What misconceptions exist?
  • What emotional triggers influence their choices?


Research helps me understand the gap between what brands think people want and what people actually care about.

03 — Creative Thinking

Finding connections where others see unrelated things.

One of my favourite creative exercises is connecting completely unrelated objects, concepts, or experiences.

For example: A bookshelf and an elevator.

At first glance, they have nothing in common. But when I look closer:

  • A bookshelf takes you somewhere mentally.
  • An elevator takes you somewhere physically.
  • Both begin with an opening.
  • Both create access.
  • Both move people from one place to another.
  • Both represent possibility.
  • Both can change your perspective depending on where they take you.


By identifying shared qualities between unrelated things, I train myself to look beyond the obvious. This approach often leads to unexpected campaign ideas, visual metaphors, and brand narratives.

I believe creativity is less about waiting for inspiration and more about learning how to see patterns.

04 — Strategy

Turning insights into direction.

Once I understand the audience and uncover the insight, I focus on creating a strategy that gives the brand a clear place in people's minds.

I look at:

  • What competitors are doing
  • What everyone else is saying
  • What conversations already exist
  • What opportunities are being ignored
  • What makes the brand genuinely different


I pay attention to trends, but I don't believe every brand should jump on every trend. A trend only matters if it supports the brand's personality and goals.

I focus on:

  • Brand Positioning: Where does the brand belong in the market?
  • USP Development: What can this brand own that competitors can't?
  • Content Direction: What stories should the brand tell consistently?
  • Platform Strategy: How should the brand behave differently on Instagram, LinkedIn, YouTube, or other platforms?
  • Brand Voice: What should the brand sound like when it speaks?
  • Cultural Relevance: How can the brand stay current without losing its identity?


My goal is to build brands that feel intentional, not reactive.

06 — Execution & Learning

Great work doesn't end when it's published.

Once a campaign or piece of content goes live, I pay attention to what happens next.

  • How did people respond?
  • What conversations did it create?
  • What performed well?
  • What didn't connect?
  • What can be improved?


I believe every project leaves behind insights that can make the next one better. Marketing isn't just about creating. It's about observing, adapting, and continuously learning.
